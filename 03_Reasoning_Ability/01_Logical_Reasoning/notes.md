# 📝 Logical Reasoning - Concept & Short Tricks

> **Concept Definition**:
> Logical Reasoning evaluates your skill to deduce sequences, classify shapes/analogy links, complete letter series, and spot anomalies in structures.

## 📊 Key Logic / Concept Table

| Reasoning Concept | Analytical Rule |
| :--- | :--- |
| Difference Analysis | Check if series grows by arithmetic differences (+3, +5, +7) or geometric ratios (x2, x3). |
| Alternating Series | Check alternate terms separately if the series oscillates up and down. |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Find next term in series: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Check difference between terms: 6-2=4, 12-6=6, 20-12=8, 30-20=10.
Step 2: Difference increases by 2 each step: 4, 6, 8, 10.
Step 3: Next diff is 12. Next term = 30 + 12 = 42.

### Example 2:
**Question**: Identify the odd one out: 27, 64, 125, 144.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Inspect term types. 27 = 3^3, 64 = 4^3, 125 = 5^3. These are perfect cubes.
Step 2: 144 = 12^2, which is a perfect square, not a cube.
Step 3: Odd one out is 144.

### Example 3:
**Question**: Complete analogy: CPU : Computer :: Heart : ?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Understand relationship. CPU is the central processing unit, the 'brain' that runs the computer.
Step 2: Apply to target: Heart is the central pumping organ of a Human body.
Step 3: Answer: Human / Human Body.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Overlooking prime numbers in series differences. If differences are 2, 3, 5, 7, the next difference is 11 (prime), not 9.

## 💡 Clue / Shortcut Trick

🔥 **Shortcut**: Check options parity. Many series questions can be filtered out by looking at odd/even sequences.
