# 📝 Data Interpretation - Concept & Short Tricks

> **Concept Definition**:
> Data Interpretation tests your quantitative and reasoning skill by demanding that you extract and analyze data from tables, line graphs, bar charts, and pie charts.

## 📊 Key Logic / Concept Table

| Reasoning Concept | Analytical Rule |
| :--- | :--- |
| Percentage Growth | $\text{Growth \%} = \frac{\text{New} - \text{Old}}{\text{Old}} \times 100$ |
| Ratio of A to B | $Ratio = \frac{\text{Sum of A}}{\text{Sum of B}}$ |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: A company sells products in 2021 (100 units) and 2022 (150 units). Find percentage growth.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Change = 150 - 100 = 50.
Step 2: Growth % = (50 / 100) * 100 = 50%.
Step 3: Growth is 50%.

### Example 2:
**Question**: In a pie chart, a sector represents 20% of total sales. Find its central angle.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Total circle is 360 degrees, which is 100%.
Step 2: Central angle = $20\% \text{ of } 360 = 0.20 \times 360 = 72^{\circ}$.
Step 3: Central angle is 72 degrees.

### Example 3:
**Question**: Table shows sales of Branch A: 10, 20, 30 and Branch B: 20, 20, 20 over 3 years. Find ratio of total sales of A to B.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Total A = 10 + 20 + 30 = 60.
Step 2: Total B = 20 + 20 + 20 = 60.
Step 3: Ratio A:B = 60:60 = 1:1.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Confusing 'percentage point change' with 'percentage change'. For example, if a rate increases from 10% to 15%, it is a 5 percentage point increase, but a 50% percentage increase.

## 💡 Clue / Shortcut Trick
