# 📝 Blood Relations - Comprehensive Reasoning Notes

> **Concept Definition**:
> Blood Relations tests family trees and relations. It covers direct statements, coded relations (e.g., A+B means A is father of B), and pointing portraits. Drawing ASCII family trees is the standard way to solve these.

| **Rule 1** | Use clear gender markers: [+] for male, [-] for female, and [?] if unknown. | Analytical Layout |

## ✍️ Worked Examples

### Example 1:
A is the father of B. C is the mother of B. D is the brother of A. How is D related to C?

A) A) Brother
B) B) Brother-in-law
C) C) Father
D) D) Cousin

**Answer**: `B`

**Step-by-Step Logic**:
1. Draw family tree:
   A[+] = C[-]
     |
     B
   D[+] - A[+]
2. A is husband of C. D is brother of A.
3. Therefore, D is brother-in-law of C. Option B.
