# 📝 Blood Relations - Concept & Short Tricks

> **Concept Definition**:
> Blood Relations tests your capability to trace relationship hierarchies across generations using family data or coded symbols.

## 📊 Key Logic / Concept Table

| Reasoning Concept | Analytical Rule |
| :--- | :--- |
| Generation Gap | Determine differences in generations. Parents (+1), Siblings (0), Grandparents (+2). |
| Coded Rule | If 'A+B' means A is father of B, draw a vertical line from A(+) to B. |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Pointing to a photograph, a man says: 'She is the daughter of my grandfather's only son.' How is the lady related to the man?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Trace from the back. 'My grandfather's only son' is the man's Father.
Step 2: 'Daughter of my father' is the man's Sister.
Step 3: Lady is the Sister of the man.

### Example 2:
**Question**: If A is husband of B, C is daughter of A, D is brother of C. How is D related to B?

**Solution**:
Step 1: A(+) = B(-). (A is husband, B is wife).
Step 2: C(-) is child below A & B.
Step 3: D(+) is sibling of C, so D(+) is also child of A & B.
Step 4: Since D is male (+), D is B's Son.

### Example 3:
**Question**: A is brother of B, who is father of C. How is A related to C?

**Solution**:
Step 1: B(+) is father of C. A(+) is brother of B.
Step 2: A is uncle to C.
Step 3: Answer: Uncle.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Assuming the gender of a person based on their name (e.g. assuming 'Kiran' is female or 'Raju' is male). Never assign gender unless explicitly stated or derived from relationships (e.g. 'husband').

## 💡 Clue / Shortcut Trick

🔥 **Shortcut**: Convert sentences into relations on yourself ('my father's brother is my uncle') to solve pointing-to photos in 20 seconds.
