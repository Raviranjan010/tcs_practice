# 📝 Clocks & Calendars - Comprehensive Reasoning Notes

> **Concept Definition**:
> Clocks & Calendars covers angle calculations between hands, clock gains/losses, and calendar day calculations using the odd days method (leap years vs non-leap years).

## 📊 Logic & Analytical Rules

| Rule Index | Analytical Principle | Diagram / Application |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rule 1** | Angle between hour and minute hands: theta = |30H - 5.5M|. | Analytical Layout |

## ✍️ Worked Examples

### Example 1:
What is the angle between the hour and minute hands at 3:40?

A) A) 120 degrees
B) B) 130 degrees
C) C) 140 degrees
D) D) 150 degrees

**Answer**: `B`

**Step-by-Step Logic**:
1. Apply clock angle formula: theta = |30 * H - 11/2 * M|.
2. H = 3, M = 40.
3. theta = |30 * 3 - 5.5 * 40| = |90 - 220| = 130 degrees.
4. The angle is 130 degrees. Option B.

### Example 2:
If 1st January 2023 was a Sunday, what day of the week was 15th March 2023?

A) A) Monday
B) B) Tuesday
C) C) Wednesday
D) D) Thursday

**Answer**: `C`

**Step-by-Step Logic**:
1. Count odd days from Jan 1 to Mar 15:
   - Remaining days in Jan = 30 days (Jan has 31, exclude Jan 1)
   - Feb (non-leap year 2023) = 28 days
   - Mar = 15 days
2. Total days = 30 + 28 + 15 = 73 days.
3. Odd days = 73 mod 7 = 3 odd days.
4. Sunday + 3 days = Wednesday.
5. Therefore, it was Wednesday. Option C.
