## [TCS NQT] [2023] Question 1
In a 6-floor building, 6 persons (P, Q, R, S, T, U) live on different floors. The ground floor is 1, and the top is 6. P lives on an even floor. Q lives immediately above S. T lives on floor 5. U lives on floor 1. Where does R live?

A) A) Floor 3
B) B) Floor 4
C) C) Floor 2
D) D) Floor 6

**Answer**: `D`

### Explanation:
1. Let's list the floors: 6, 5, 4, 3, 2, 1.
2. T lives on 5. U lives on 1: [6:?, 5:T, 4:?, 3:?, 2:?, 1:U].
3. Q lives immediately above S. This means Q, S occupy consecutive free floors. The only consecutive free floors are 3, 2 or 4, 3. Let's test.
4. P lives on an even floor. The even floors are 6, 4, 2. If P lives on 4: [6:?, 5:T, 4:P, 3:?, 2:?, 1:U]. Then consecutive floors for Q, S must be 3, 2: [6:?, 5:T, 4:P, 3:Q, 2:S, 1:U].
5. The only remaining floor for R is 6: [6:R, 5:T, 4:P, 3:Q, 2:S, 1:U].
6. All clues verified. R lives on floor 6. Option D.
