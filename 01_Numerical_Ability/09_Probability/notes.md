# 📝 Probability - Comprehensive Study Notes

> **Concept Definition**:
> Likelihood of event occurrences. Includes coin tosses, dice rolls, playing card selections, ball draws from bags, and conditional/Bayes probability.

## 📊 Formula Reference Table

| Concept / Formula | Equation | Description / Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Probability of Event E** | `$Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes$` | P(E) = n(E) / n(S) |
| **Addition Rule** | `$P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A and B)$` | For any two events A and B |
| **Independent Events** | `$P(A and B) = P(A) * P(B)$` | Events do not affect each other |
| **Conditional Probability** | `$P(A|B) = P(A and B) / P(B)$` | Probability of A given B has occurred |

## ✍️ 5 Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Two dice are thrown together. Find probability that sum is 8.

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
Total outcomes = 36. Favorable: (2,6), (3,5), (4,4), (5,3), (6,2) -> 5. P = 5/36.

**Correct Answer**: `5/36`

### Example 2:
**Question**: A card is drawn from a pack of 52. Find probability it is a king or heart.

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
Kings = 4, Hearts = 13, King of Hearts = 1. Favorable = 4 + 13 - 1 = 16. P = 16/52 = 4/13.

**Correct Answer**: `4/13`

## ⚠️ EXAM TRAPS (TCS Specific)

1. **Double counting overlapping cases in card or dice problems.**
2. **Assuming events are independent when they are dependent (without replacement).**

## 🔥 60-Second Shortcuts & Speed Tricks

- **Trick 1**: In 2-dice throws, the sum distribution forms a symmetric pyramid from 2 (1 case) to 7 (6 cases) back to 12 (1 case).

## 🧠 Memory Aid & Mnemonic
**Mnemonic**: P(A|B) = P(A intersect B) / P(B).

To build mathematical speed for the NQT exam, one must master prime factorizations. A number N decomposed into prime bases is the gateway to calculating divisibility properties, divisors count, divisors sum, and totient calculations.
Always remember to solve problems by converting variables to their lowest prime bases (2, 3, 5, 7, 11...). This renders complex divisions into simple exponent arithmetic operations.
