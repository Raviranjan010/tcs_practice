## [2024] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Digital]
**Question 1**:
What is the probability that a leap year selected at random will contain 53 Sundays?

**Options**:
- A) 1/7
- B) 2/7
- C) 3/7
- D) 5/7

**Correct Answer**: **B) 2/7**

### Explanation:
A leap year has 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 odd days.
These 2 odd days can be: (Mon, Tue), (Tue, Wed), (Wed, Thu), (Thu, Fri), (Fri, Sat), (Sat, Sun), or (Sun, Mon).
Out of these 7 pairs, 2 pairs contain Sunday: (Sat, Sun) and (Sun, Mon).
Therefore, the probability of getting 53 Sundays is 2/7.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Leap year always has 2 odd days. Thus, any day having 53 occurrences has a probability of 2/7. A non-leap year has 1 odd day, so its probability is 1/7.

## [2023] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Ninja]
**Question 2**:
What is the value of the expression evaluated under standard arithmetic conventions?

**Options**:
- A) 1
- B) 0
- C) -1
- D) 2

**Correct Answer**: **A) 1**

### Explanation:
Under standard conventions, any simplified ratio or fraction where numerator equals denominator yields 1.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Check for symmetry or cancellation. Symmetrical expressions cancel out to 1.
