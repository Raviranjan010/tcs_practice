# 📝 Permutation & Combination - Comprehensive Study Notes

> **Concept Definition**:
> Counting arrangements (permutations) and selections (combinations). Includes circular permutations, identical objects, and partitioning.

## 📊 Formula Reference Table

| Concept / Formula | Equation | Description / Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Permutations of n distinct objects taken r at a time** | `$n! / (n-r)!$` | nPr |
| **Combinations of n distinct objects taken r at a time** | `$n! / [r! * (n-r)!]$` | nCr |
| **Circular Permutations** | `$(n-1)!$` | For n distinct objects arranged in a circle |
| **Permutations with identical objects** | `$n! / (p! * q!)$` | p, q are frequencies of repeated objects |

## ✍️ 5 Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Find number of ways to arrange letters of word 'LEADER'.

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
Total letters = 6. 'E' repeats twice. Ways = 6! / 2! = 720 / 2 = 360.

**Correct Answer**: `360`

### Example 2:
**Question**: In how many ways can a committee of 3 be chosen from 5 men and 4 women such that it has 2 men and 1 woman?

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
Ways = 5C2 * 4C1 = 10 * 4 = 40.

**Correct Answer**: `40`

## ⚠️ EXAM TRAPS (TCS Specific)

1. **Using permutation when order does not matter (combination), or vice versa.**
2. **Forgetting circular necklace arrangements have (n-1)! / 2 ways.**

## 🔥 60-Second Shortcuts & Speed Tricks

- **Trick 1**: nCr = nC(n-r). E.g., 10C8 = 10C2 = 45.

## 🧠 Memory Aid & Mnemonic
**Mnemonic**: Permutation = Order matters. Combination = Grouping matters.

This topic forms the basis of many complex quantitative aptitude problems, especially in data interpretation, modular arithmetic in coding, and sequence generation.

Always remember to solve problems by converting variables to their lowest prime bases (2, 3, 5, 7, 11...). This renders complex divisions into simple exponent arithmetic operations.
