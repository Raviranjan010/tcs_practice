# 📝 Averages & Mixtures - Concept & Shortcuts

> **Concept Definition**:
> Averages represent the central value of a data set. Mixtures and Alligations is a rule that enables us to find the ratio in which two or more ingredients at given prices must be mixed to produce a mixture of a specified average price.

## 📊 Formula / Trick Table

| Concept / Formula | Mathematical Expression |
| :--- | :--- |
| Basic Average | $\text{Average} = \frac{\text{Sum of terms}}{\text{Number of terms}}$ |
| Weighted Average | $A_{weighted} = \frac{n_1 A_1 + n_2 A_2}{n_1 + n_2}$ |
| Alligation Rule | $\frac{\text{Qty of cheaper}}{\text{Qty of dearer}} = \frac{\text{Dearer price} - \text{Mean price}}{\text{Mean price} - \text{Cheaper price}}$ |
| Replacement Formula | $FC = IC \left(1 - \frac{x}{V}\right)^n$ where $FC$=Final Conc., $IC$=Initial Conc., $x$=qty replaced, $V$=volume, $n$=cycles. |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: The average weight of 8 men is increased by 1.5 kg when a new man replaces one who weighs 65 kg. What is the weight of the new man?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Increase in weight = $8 \times 1.5 = 12$ kg.
Step 2: Weight of new man = Weight of replaced man + Total Increase.
Step 3: Weight of new man = $65 + 12 = 77$ kg.

### Example 2:
**Question**: In what ratio must tea worth Rs. 60 per kg be mixed with tea worth Rs. 65 per kg so that the mixture is worth Rs. 62 per kg?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Cheaper Price (C) = 60, Dearer Price (D) = 65, Mean Price (M) = 62.
Step 2: Apply Alligation rule:
Ratio = $(D - M) / (M - C) = (65 - 62) / (62 - 60)$.
Step 3: Ratio = 3 / 2.
Step 4: The ratio is 3:2.

### Example 3:
**Question**: A jar contains 40L of pure milk. 4L of milk is replaced with water. This process is repeated one more time. Find the final quantity of milk.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Initial Concentration ($IC$) = 40, replaced quantity ($x$) = 4, Total volume ($V$) = 40, cycles ($n$) = 2.
Step 2: Final milk = $40 \left(1 - \frac{4}{40}\right)^2$.
Step 3: Final milk = $40 \left(0.9\right)^2 = 40 \times 0.81 = 32.4$ liters.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: In replacement questions, students often forget that the total volume ($V$) remains constant because water is added to replace the milk, but the milk concentration decreases. They also sometimes miss subtracting the final milk from total volume if the question asks for the final water quantity.

## 💡 Shortcut / Vedic Trick

🔥 **Vedic Shortcut**: The Alligation Cross is your best friend. Draw it out to solve average/mixture problems in under 30 seconds without solving linear equations.
