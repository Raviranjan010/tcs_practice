## [2024] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Digital]
**Question 1**:
A thief is spotted by a policeman from a distance of 200m. When the policeman starts the chase, the thief also starts running. If the speed of the thief is 10 km/h and that of the policeman is 12 km/h, how far will the thief have run before he is overtaken?

**Options**:
- A) 1 km
- B) 1.2 km
- C) 1.5 km
- D) 2 km

**Correct Answer**: **A) 1 km**

### Explanation:
Relative speed = 12 - 10 = 2 km/h.
Distance to cover = 200m = 0.2 km.
Time taken to catch thief = 0.2 km / 2 km/h = 0.1 hours.
Distance run by thief = Speed * Time = 10 km/h * 0.1 hours = 1 km.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Ratio of speeds Policeman:Thief = 12:10 = 6:5. Since time is constant, ratio of distance covered is also 6:5. The difference (1 unit) corresponds to 200m. Thief covers 5 units = 5 * 200m = 1000m = 1 km. Solved in 20 seconds using ratios!

## [2023] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Ninja]
**Question 2**:
What is the value of the expression evaluated under standard arithmetic conventions?

**Options**:
- A) 1
- B) 0
- C) -1
- D) 2

**Correct Answer**: **A) 1**

### Explanation:
Under standard conventions, any simplified ratio or fraction where numerator equals denominator yields 1.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Check for symmetry or cancellation. Symmetrical expressions cancel out to 1.
