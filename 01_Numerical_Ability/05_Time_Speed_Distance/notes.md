# 📝 Time, Speed & Distance - Concept & Shortcuts

> **Concept Definition**:
> Time, Speed, and Distance (TSD) covers the relationship between moving objects. Sub-topics include relative speed (trains meeting), average speed, boats moving in streams (upstream/downstream), and circular tracks.

## 📊 Formula / Trick Table

| Concept / Formula | Mathematical Expression |
| :--- | :--- |
| Basic formula | $D = S \times T$ |
| Relative Speed (Trains) | $S_{rel} = S_1 + S_2$ (opposite), $S_{rel} = |S_1 - S_2|$ (same direction) |
| Boats & Streams | $U_{down} = B + S$, $U_{up} = B - S$ where $B$ is boat speed, $S$ is stream speed. |
| Boat speed in still water | $B = \frac{U_{down} + U_{up}}{2}$ |
| Stream speed | $S = \frac{U_{down} - U_{up}}{2}$ |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: A train 150m long passes a telegraph post in 9 seconds. Find the speed of the train in km/h.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Distance = train length = 150m. Time = 9 seconds.
Step 2: Speed = $150 / 9 = 50 / 3$ m/s.
Step 3: Convert to km/h: Speed in km/h = $\frac{50}{3} \times \frac{18}{5} = 10 \times 6 = 60$ km/h.
Step 4: The speed of the train is 60 km/h.

### Example 2:
**Question**: A boat goes 24 km downstream in 2 hours and 16 km upstream in 2 hours. Find the speed of the boat in still water.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Downstream Speed ($U_{down}$) = 24 / 2 = 12 km/h.
Step 2: Upstream Speed ($U_{up}$) = 16 / 2 = 8 km/h.
Step 3: Speed of boat in still water = $\frac{U_{down} + U_{up}}{2} = \frac{12 + 8}{2} = 10$ km/h.
Step 4: Speed of boat is 10 km/h.

### Example 3:
**Question**: A man walks at 4 km/h instead of 3 km/h and covers 5 km more distance in the same time. Find the actual distance.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Let the time be $T$ hours.
Step 2: Distance at 3 km/h = $3T$, distance at 4 km/h = $4T$.
Step 3: Given $4T - 3T = 5 \implies T = 5$ hours.
Step 4: Actual distance = $3T = 15$ km.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: A common mistake is adding the speeds of boats directly to calculate downstream/upstream times. Make sure to subtract the stream speed for upstream and add it for downstream.

## 💡 Shortcut / Vedic Trick

🔥 **Vedic Shortcut**: When the distance is constant, speed and time are inversely proportional ($S_1/S_2 = T_2/T_1$). Use ratios to solve without calculating absolute distances.
