# 📝 Percentages - Concept & Shortcuts

> **Concept Definition**:
> Percentages represent ratios as fractions of 100. They are essential for comparing relative values, analyzing population growth, salary changes, and form the basis for Profit/Loss and Data Interpretation.

## 📊 Formula / Trick Table

| Concept / Formula | Mathematical Expression |
| :--- | :--- |
| Percentage Increase/Decrease | $\text{Change \%} = \frac{\text{Difference}}{\text{Original Value}} \times 100$ |
| Successive Percentage Change | $\text{Net \%} = a + b + \frac{ab}{100}$ |
| Product Constancy | If price increases by $x\%$, consumption must decrease by $\frac{x}{100+x} \times 100\%$ to keep expenditure constant. |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: The price of petrol increases by 25%. By how much percent must a driver reduce consumption so that the expenditure remains unchanged?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Let original price be 100 and consumption be 100. Total expenditure = 10000.
Step 2: New price = 125. New consumption = $C$. New expenditure = $125 \times C = 10000$.
Step 3: $C = 10000 / 125 = 80$.
Step 4: Reduction in consumption = $100 - 80 = 20$%. 
Shortcut Formula: $\frac{x}{100+x} \times 100 = \frac{25}{125} \times 100 = 20\%$.

### Example 2:
**Question**: If a student's marks increase by 20% and then decrease by 10%, what is the net percentage change?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Let the original marks be 100.
Step 2: After 20% increase: $100 \times 1.20 = 120$.
Step 3: After 10% decrease: $120 \times 0.90 = 108$.
Step 4: Net change = $108 - 100 = 8$\% increase.
Shortcut: $a=20, b=-10 \implies 20 - 10 + \frac{20 \times (-10)}{100} = 10 - 2 = 8\%$.

### Example 3:
**Question**: In an election, a candidate gets 60% of the total valid votes. 20% of the votes were declared invalid. If the total number of votes was 7500, find the number of valid votes the candidate got.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Total votes = 7500.
Step 2: Valid votes = 80% of 7500 = $7500 \times 0.8 = 6000$.
Step 3: Candidate's votes = 60% of valid votes = $6000 \times 0.6 = 3600$.
Step 4: The candidate got 3600 votes.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Students often add successive percentages linearly (e.g. thinking a 10% increase followed by a 10% decrease results in 0% change). Remember that the second percentage is calculated on the updated value, resulting in a net decrease ($10 - 10 - 1 = -1\%$).

## 💡 Shortcut / Vedic Trick

🔥 **Vedic Shortcut**: Memorize the fraction table! An increase of $1/n$ is balanced by a decrease of $1/(n+1)$ to keep the product constant.
