## [2024] [DIFFICULTY: Easy] [CATEGORY: Ninja]
**Question 1**:
If A's income is 40% less than B's income, by how much percent is B's income more than A's?

**Options**:
- A) 40%
- B) 66.67%
- C) 60%
- D) 33.33%

**Correct Answer**: **B) 66.67%**

### Explanation:
Let B's income = 100. Then A's income = 60.
B's income is more than A's by: (40 / 60) * 100 = 2/3 * 100 = 66.67%.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Income of A is 40% (2/5) less. Apply reciprocal rule: a decrease of $x/(x+y)$ is balanced by an increase of $x/y$. Here, decrease is 2/5, so increase is 2/(5-2) = 2/3 = 66.67%.

## [2023] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Ninja]
**Question 2**:
What is the value of the expression evaluated under standard arithmetic conventions?

**Options**:
- A) 1
- B) 0
- C) -1
- D) 2

**Correct Answer**: **A) 1**

### Explanation:
Under standard conventions, any simplified ratio or fraction where numerator equals denominator yields 1.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Check for symmetry or cancellation. Symmetrical expressions cancel out to 1.
