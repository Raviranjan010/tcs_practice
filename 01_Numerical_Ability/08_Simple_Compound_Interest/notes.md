# 📝 Simple & Compound Interest - Concept & Shortcuts

> **Concept Definition**:
> Simple Interest (SI) is calculated only on the principal amount. Compound Interest (CI) is interest calculated on the principal plus accumulated interest of previous periods, representing exponential growth.

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Find the compound interest on Rs. 10000 for 2 years at 10% per annum compounded annually.

**Solution**:
Step 1: P = 10000, R = 10%, n = 2.
Step 2: $A = 10000 \left(1 + \frac{10}{100}\right)^2 = 10000 \times (1.1)^2 = 10000 \times 1.21 = 12100$.
Step 3: $CI = A - P = 12100 - 10000 = 2100$.
Step 4: Compound Interest is Rs. 2100.

### Example 2:
**Question**: The difference between simple and compound interest on a sum of money for 2 years at 5% per annum is Rs. 25. Find the principal.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Difference = Rs. 25, R = 5%, n = 2.
Step 2: Use 2-year difference formula: $Diff = P \left(\frac{R}{100}\right)^2$.
Step 3: $25 = P \left(\frac{5}{100}\right)^2 \implies 25 = P \times \frac{25}{10000}$.
Step 4: $P = 10000$. The principal is Rs. 10000.

### Example 3:
**Question**: A sum of money doubles itself in 4 years at simple interest. In how many years will it become 8 times itself?

**Solution**:
Step 1: For SI, a sum becomes $N$ times in time $T = (N-1) \times T_{\text{double}}$.
Step 2: Let principal be $P$. Interest earned in 4 years is $P$ (since it doubles). So rate $R = 100 / 4 = 25\%$.
Step 3: To become 8 times, the interest earned must be $7P$.
Step 4: Time = $\frac{7P \times 100}{P \times 25} = 7 \times 4 = 28$ years.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: In compound interest questions with half-yearly compounding, students often forget to divide the rate by 2 and double the time periods. Make sure to update parameters before plugging them into the formula.

## 💡 Shortcut / Vedic Trick

🔥 **Vedic Shortcut**: The Tree Method: Instead of complex formulas, calculate interest year-by-year on principal, and then interest-on-interest to build the tree. This simplifies 3-year CI calculations.
