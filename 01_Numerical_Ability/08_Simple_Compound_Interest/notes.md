# 📝 Simple & Compound Interest - Comprehensive Study Notes

> **Concept Definition**:
> Simple Interest (SI) where interest is calculated only on principal, and Compound Interest (CI) where interest is calculated on accumulated amount. Includes compounding periods and installment calculations.

## 📊 Formula Reference Table

| Concept / Formula | Equation | Description / Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Simple Interest** | `$P*R*T / 100$` | Interest only on principal |
| **Compound Amount** | `$P * (1 + R/100)^T$` | Compounded annually |
| **Difference CI - SI (2 yr)** | `$P * (R/100)^2$` | Difference for 2 years |
| **Difference CI - SI (3 yr)** | `$P * (R/100)^2 * (300 + R)/100$` | Difference for 3 years |
| **Half-yearly compounding** | `$A = P * (1 + R/200)^(2T)$` | Rate halved, time doubled |

## ✍️ 5 Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Find SI on Rs.1000 at 10% per annum for 3 years.

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
SI = 1000 * 10 * 3 / 100 = Rs.300.

**Correct Answer**: `Rs. 300`

### Example 2:
**Question**: Find CI on Rs.1000 at 10% per annum for 2 years compounded annually.

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
Amount = 1000 * (1.1)^2 = 1210. CI = 1210 - 1000 = Rs.210.

**Correct Answer**: `Rs. 210`

### Example 3:
**Question**: The difference between CI and SI on a sum for 2 years at 5% is Rs.25. Find principal.

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
Difference = P*(5/100)^2 = 25 => P * 1/400 = 25 => P = 10000.

**Correct Answer**: `Rs. 10000`

## 🔥 60-Second Shortcuts & Speed Tricks

- **Trick 1**: Effective rate for CI: 2 years at R% is 2R + (R^2)/100 %.
