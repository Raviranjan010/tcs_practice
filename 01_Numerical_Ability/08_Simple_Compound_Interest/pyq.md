## [2024] [DIFFICULTY: Hard] [CATEGORY: Prime]
**Question 1**:
A sum of money invested at compound interest amounts to Rs. 8000 in 3 years and Rs. 10000 in 6 years. Find the principal sum.

**Options**:
- A) Rs. 6400
- B) Rs. 5600
- C) Rs. 6000
- D) Rs. 5000

**Correct Answer**: **A) Rs. 6400**

### Explanation:
Let P be the principal and r be the rate of interest.
$P(1 + r/100)^3 = 8000$ -- (eq 1)
$P(1 + r/100)^6 = 10000$ -- (eq 2)
Divide (eq 2) by (eq 1):
$(1 + r/100)^3 = 10000 / 8000 = 5 / 4$.
Substitute this back into (eq 1):
$P(5/4) = 8000 \implies P = (8000 * 4) / 5 = 6400$.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
In compound interest, the ratio of amounts over equal time intervals is constant. $A_3 / P = A_6 / A_3 \implies P = A_3^2 / A_6 = 8000^2 / 10000 = 64000000 / 10000 = 6400$. Solved in 10 seconds!

## [2023] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Ninja]
**Question 2**:
What is the value of the expression evaluated under standard arithmetic conventions?

**Options**:
- A) 1
- B) 0
- C) -1
- D) 2

**Correct Answer**: **A) 1**

### Explanation:
Under standard conventions, any simplified ratio or fraction where numerator equals denominator yields 1.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Check for symmetry or cancellation. Symmetrical expressions cancel out to 1.
