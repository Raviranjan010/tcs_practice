# 📝 Profit & Loss - Concept & Shortcuts

> **Concept Definition**:
> Profit and Loss deals with business transactions, determining the financial outcome of buying and selling items. Key elements include Cost Price (CP), Selling Price (SP), Marked Price (MP), and Discount.

## 📊 Formula / Trick Table

| Concept / Formula | Mathematical Expression |
| :--- | :--- |
| Profit Percentage | $\text{Profit \%} = \frac{SP - CP}{CP} \times 100$ |
| Loss Percentage | $\text{Loss \%} = \frac{CP - SP}{CP} \times 100$ |
| Mark-up Percentage | $\text{Mark-up \%} = \frac{MP - CP}{CP} \times 100$ |
| Discount Percentage | $\text{Discount \%} = \frac{MP - SP}{MP} \times 100$ |
| Relation between CP, MP, Discount & Profit | $\frac{MP}{CP} = \frac{100 + \text{Profit \%}}{100 - \text{Discount \%}}$ |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: A shopkeeper marks his goods 20% above the cost price and allows a discount of 10%. Find his gain or loss percent.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Let the Cost Price (CP) be 100.
Step 2: Marked Price (MP) = 120 (since mark-up is 20%).
Step 3: Selling Price (SP) = MP - Discount = $120 - 10\% \text{ of } 120 = 120 - 12 = 108$.
Step 4: Profit % = $\frac{SP - CP}{CP} \times 100 = \frac{108 - 100}{100} \times 100 = 8\%$.

### Example 2:
**Question**: By selling an item for Rs. 720, a shopkeeper incurs a loss of 10%. At what price should he sell it to gain 20%?

**Solution**:
Step 1: SP = 720, Loss = 10%. So, $720 = 90\% \text{ of CP} \implies CP = 720 / 0.9 = 800$.
Step 2: To gain 20%, target SP must be 120% of CP.
Step 3: Target SP = $800 \times 1.20 = 960$.
Step 4: He must sell it for Rs. 960.

### Example 3:
**Question**: A dishonest dealer claims to sell his goods at cost price but uses a weight of 900g instead of 1kg. Find his profit percentage.

**Solution**:
Step 1: The dealer charges the price of 1000g (SP equivalent) but delivers only 900g (CP equivalent).
Step 2: Profit = 1000g - 900g = 100g.
Step 3: Profit % = $\frac{\text{Error}}{\text{True Weight} - \text{Error}} \times 100 = \frac{100}{900} \times 100 = 11.11\%$.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: When dealing with dishonest dealers, students often calculate profit percentage on the standard weight (e.g. 1000g) rather than the actual weight delivered (e.g. 900g). Remember that profit is always relative to what the dealer actually spends (CP), which is the weight delivered.

## 💡 Shortcut / Vedic Trick

🔥 **Vedic Shortcut**: When two items are sold at the same price, one at $x\%$ profit and the other at $x\%$ loss, there is always a net loss of $\frac{x^2}{100}\%$.
