## [TCS NQT] [2023] [Ninja] [Medium] Question 1

A man sells two articles for Rs. 990 each, gaining 10% on one and losing 10% on the other. What is his net profit or loss percentage?

A) 1% profit
B) 1% loss
C) No profit no loss
D) 2% loss

**Answer**: `B`

### Explanation:
1. When SP of two items is same and profit% on one equals loss% on other (say x%), there is always a loss.
2. Net loss% = (x^2)/100 = (10^2)/100 = 100/100 = 1%.
3. Hence, the man incurs a 1% loss.

## [TCS NQT] [2023] [Ninja] [Medium] Question 3

A shopkeeper marks his goods 20% above the cost price. He sells half the stock at the marked price, one-quarter at a 20% discount, and the rest at a 40% discount. Find his overall profit or loss percentage.

A) 2% profit
B) 5% profit
C) 2% loss
D) No profit no loss

**Answer**: `A`

### Explanation:
1. Let total stock be 4 units, CP per unit = 100. Total CP = 400.
2. Marked Price (MP) per unit = 120.
3. Sales details:
   - 2 units sold at MP: 2 * 120 = 240.
   - 1 unit sold at 20% discount on MP: 120 * 0.8 = 96.
   - 1 unit sold at 40% discount on MP: 120 * 0.6 = 72.
4. Total SP = 240 + 96 + 72 = 408.
5. Overall Profit = 408 - 400 = 8. Profit% = (8 / 400) * 100 = 2% profit.

## [TCS NQT] [2023] [Ninja] [Medium] Question 9

A trader sells an article at a profit of 20%. Had he bought it for 10% less and sold it for Rs. 18 less, he would have gained 30%. Find the cost price of the article.

A) Rs. 600
B) Rs. 500
C) Rs. 400
D) Rs. 300

**Answer**: `A`

### Explanation:
1. Let original CP = 100x. Original SP = 120x.
2. New CP = 90x (10% less).
3. New SP = 120x - 18.
4. New profit = 30%. So New SP = 1.30 * New CP => 120x - 18 = 1.30 * 90x.
5. 120x - 18 = 117x => 3x = 18 => x = 6.
6. Original CP = 100x = 600. So Cost Price is Rs. 600.
