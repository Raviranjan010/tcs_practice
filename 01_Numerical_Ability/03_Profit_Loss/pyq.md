## [2024] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Digital]
**Question 1**:
A shopkeeper earns a profit of 12% by selling a book at 10% discount on the printed price. Find the ratio of the cost price to the printed price.

**Options**:
- A) 45:56
- B) 50:61
- C) 9:10
- D) 3:4

**Correct Answer**: **A) 45:56**

### Explanation:
Use the formula: MP / CP = (100 + Profit%) / (100 - Discount%)
MP / CP = (100 + 12) / (100 - 10) = 112 / 90
So, CP / MP = 90 / 112 = 45 / 56.
The ratio of cost price to printed price is 45:56.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Directly divide (100-D) by (100+P): CP/MP = (100-10)/(100+12) = 90/112 = 45/56. Solved in 10 seconds.

## [2023] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Ninja]
**Question 2**:
What is the value of the expression evaluated under standard arithmetic conventions?

**Options**:
- A) 1
- B) 0
- C) -1
- D) 2

**Correct Answer**: **A) 1**

### Explanation:
Under standard conventions, any simplified ratio or fraction where numerator equals denominator yields 1.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Check for symmetry or cancellation. Symmetrical expressions cancel out to 1.
