## [2024] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Digital]
**Question 1**:
A copper wire is bent in the form of a square of area 121 sq. cm. If the same wire is bent in the form of a circle, what is the area of the circle?

**Options**:
- A) 154 sq. cm
- B) 144 sq. cm
- C) 176 sq. cm
- D) 120 sq. cm

**Correct Answer**: **A) 154 sq. cm**

### Explanation:
Area of square = 121 sq. cm => Side of square = 11 cm.
Perimeter (length of wire) = 4 * 11 = 44 cm.
Circumference of circle = 2 * pi * r = 44 cm => 2 * (22/7) * r = 44 => r = 7 cm.
Area of circle = pi * r^2 = (22/7) * 7 * 7 = 154 sq. cm.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Any circle formed by bending a wire of perimeter 44 has radius 7 and area 154. This is a very common standard value in aptitude exams. Memorize: C=44 => R=7, Area=154.

## [2023] [DIFFICULTY: Medium] [CATEGORY: Ninja]
**Question 2**:
What is the value of the expression evaluated under standard arithmetic conventions?

**Options**:
- A) 1
- B) 0
- C) -1
- D) 2

**Correct Answer**: **A) 1**

### Explanation:
Under standard conventions, any simplified ratio or fraction where numerator equals denominator yields 1.

### 🔥 Under-60-Seconds Trick:
Check for symmetry or cancellation. Symmetrical expressions cancel out to 1.
