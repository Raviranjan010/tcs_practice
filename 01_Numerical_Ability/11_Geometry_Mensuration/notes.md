# 📝 Geometry & Mensuration - Comprehensive Study Notes

> **Concept Definition**:
> Properties of lines, angles, triangles, polygons, circles, and area/perimeter/volume calculations for 13 standard shapes.

## 📊 Formula Reference Table

| Concept / Formula | Equation | Description / Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Area of Triangle** | `$1/2 * base * height$` | General formula |
| **Area of Equilateral Triangle** | `$sqrt(3)/4 * side^2$` | Equilateral case |
| **Volume of Cylinder** | `$pi * r^2 * h$` | Circular cylinder |
| **TSA of Cylinder** | `$2 * pi * r * (r + h)$` | Total surface area |

## ✍️ 5 Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Find the volume of a sphere of radius 3 cm.

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
Volume = 4/3 * pi * 3^3 = 36 * pi cubic cm.

**Correct Answer**: `36*pi`

### Example 2:
**Question**: The sides of a triangle are 3, 4, 5 cm. Find its area.

**Step-by-Step Solution**:
Right triangle with base 3, height 4. Area = 1/2 * 3 * 4 = 6 sq cm.

**Correct Answer**: `6`

## ⚠️ EXAM TRAPS (TCS Specific)

1. **Confusing Total Surface Area with Curved Surface Area.**
2. **Failing to convert height and radius to the same units before calculating volume.**

## 🔥 60-Second Shortcuts & Speed Tricks

- **Trick 1**: Pythagorean triples: (3,4,5), (5,12,13), (8,15,17), (7,24,25).

## 🧠 Memory Aid & Mnemonic
**Mnemonic**: Cylinder: V = pi*r^2*h, TSA = 2*pi*r(r+h). Cone: V = 1/3*pi*r^2*h, TSA = pi*r(r+l).

Always remember to solve problems by converting variables to their lowest prime bases (2, 3, 5, 7, 11...). This renders complex divisions into simple exponent arithmetic operations.
