# 📝 Geometry & Mensuration - Concept & Shortcuts

> **Concept Definition**:
> Geometry deals with the properties, measurement, and relationships of points, lines, angles, and surfaces. Mensuration measures geometric parameters like area, perimeter, and volume of 2D and 3D shapes.

## 📊 Formula / Trick Table

| Concept / Formula | Mathematical Expression |
| :--- | :--- |
| Area of Triangle | $A = \frac{1}{2} \times b \times h$ or $\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)}$ |
| Circle Area & Circumference | $A = \pi r^2$, $C = 2\pi r$ |
| Cylinder Volume & Surface Area | $V = \pi r^2 h$, $CSA = 2\pi r h$, $TSA = 2\pi r(r+h)$ |
| Sphere Volume & Surface Area | $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$, $SA = 4\pi r^2$ |
| Cone Volume | $V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$ |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: The radius of a cylinder is 7cm and its height is 10cm. Find its curved surface area and volume.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Radius ($r$) = 7, Height ($h$) = 10. Use $\pi = 22/7$.
Step 2: Curved Surface Area ($CSA$) = $2\pi r h = 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 10 = 440 \text{ cm}^2$.
Step 3: Volume ($V$) = $\pi r^2 h = \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 7 \times 10 = 1540 \text{ cm}^3$.

### Example 2:
**Question**: If the diagonal of a square is 10cm, find its area.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Area of square in terms of diagonal $d$ is $A = \frac{d^2}{2}$.
Step 2: $A = \frac{10^2}{2} = \frac{100}{2} = 50 \text{ cm}^2$.
Step 3: The area of the square is 50 cm^2.

### Example 3:
**Question**: Three metallic spheres of radii 3cm, 4cm, and 5cm are melted to form a single solid sphere. Find the radius of the new sphere.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Volume of new sphere = Sum of volumes of the three spheres.
Step 2: $\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi (3^3 + 4^3 + 5^3)$.
Step 3: $R^3 = 27 + 64 + 125 = 216$.
Step 4: $R = \sqrt[3]{216} = 6$ cm.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Students often make calculation mistakes with $\pi$ or confuse curved surface area with total surface area. Read carefully if the shape is closed (requires TSA) or open (requires CSA).

## 💡 Shortcut / Vedic Trick

🔥 **Vedic Shortcut**: If the side/radius of a 2D shape is increased by $x\%$, the area increases by $(2x + x^2/100)\%$. If it is a 3D shape, volume increases by $(3x + 3x^2/100 + x^3/10000)\%$.
