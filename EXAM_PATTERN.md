# 📋 TCS NQT Exam Pattern (Latest 2024-25 Version)

To crack the TCS NQT, you must understand the exact test structure, sectional cutoffs, and proctoring rules. TCS NQT is divided into two primary sections: **Foundation** (for Ninja) and **Advanced** (for Digital & Prime).

---

## ⏱️ Section-wise Structure & Time Limits

The test is conducted on the TCS iON Platform. The sections appear in a fixed sequence, and you cannot switch between sections or go back to previous questions once the time expires.

### Part A: Foundation Section (Common to all roles)
*This section determines your eligibility for the **Ninja** category and is the qualifying criteria for the Advanced section.*

| Section | Number of Questions | Time Allocated | Key Topics Covered |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **Numerical Ability** | 20 Questions | 25 Minutes | Number System, Percentages, Time & Work, Algebra, P&C |
| **Verbal Ability** | 20 Questions | 20 Minutes | Reading Comprehension, Prepositions, Error Spotting |
| **Reasoning Ability** | 20 Questions | 25 Minutes | Syllogisms, Blood Relations, Seating Arrangement, Data Interpretation |
| **Total (Foundation)** | **60 Questions** | **70 Minutes** | |

### Part B: Advanced Section (Required for Digital & Prime roles)
*If you perform exceptionally well in Part A, your Part B will be evaluated to determine your candidacy for **Digital (7 LPA)** or **Prime (9+ LPA)**.*

| Section | Number of Questions | Time Allocated | Key Topics Covered |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **Advanced Quantitative Ability** | 10 Questions | 20 Minutes | Geometry, Advanced Probability, Number Theory, Charts |
| **Advanced Reasoning Ability** | 10 Questions | 15 Minutes | Complex Puzzles, Cryptarithmetic, Multi-dimensional Data |
| **Advanced Coding** | 2 Coding Problems | 45 Minutes | Data Structures (Arrays, Strings, Stacks, Trees, DP, Graphs) |
| **Total (Advanced)** | **22 Questions** | **80 Minutes** | |

**Grand Total**: **82 Questions in 150 Minutes**

## 🎯 Sectional Cutoff Estimates

To clear the exam, you need to score above the sectional cutoffs. Since TCS does not officially release cutoffs, these estimates are based on historical candidate data.

| Category | Foundation Cutoff (Part A) | Advanced Cutoff (Part B - MCQ) | Coding Section Cutoff |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **Ninja** | 60%–65% accuracy | Not Applicable | 1 basic problem (or partial code execution) |
| **Digital** | 70%–75% accuracy | 65%–70% accuracy | 1 complete problem solved with optimal complexity |
| **Prime** | 80%+ accuracy | 80%+ accuracy | Both coding problems fully solved optimally |

## ⚠️ Negative Marking & Exam Rules

* **Negative Marking**: There is **no negative marking** in the TCS NQT. Attempt every single question!
* **Navigation Constraints**: You cannot skip questions or move backward. Once you submit an answer, it is locked.
* **Calculator**: An on-screen scientific calculator is provided. Physical calculators are strictly banned.
* **Rough Work**: One sheet of rough paper is allowed (must be shown to the camera during online proctoring).

---

## 🖥️ Online Proctoring Tips (Home-Based Exam)

If you choose the Remote/Home option instead of an In-Center test:
1. **System Check**: Run the system compatibility tool 24 hours before the exam. Ensure your webcam, mic, and internet speed (minimum 2 Mbps) are functioning properly.
2. **Environment**: Take the test in a quiet, well-lit room. Your face must be clearly visible. Background noise or someone entering the room will trigger warnings.
3. **No Tab Switching**: The TCS Secure Browser locks your screen. Any attempt to switch tabs or minimize the window will immediately terminate the exam.
4. **Eye Movement**: Avoid looking away from the screen repeatedly. The AI proctor flags frequent gaze deviations as suspicious behavior.

## 🔄 Post-NQT Recruitment Process

Once you clear the cutoffs, you will receive an interview invitation. The process consists of three rounds, often conducted sequentially on the same day:

```
┌──────────────┐      ┌──────────────┐      ┌──────────────┐      ┌──────────────┐
│  TCS NQT     │ ───> │  Technical   │ ───> │ Managerial   │ ───> │  HR Round    │
│  (Written)   │      │  Round (TR)  │      │  Round (MR)  │      │  (Salary/HR) │
└──────────────┘      └──────────────┘      └──────────────┘      └──────────────┘
```

1. **Technical Round (TR)**:
   - **Focus**: Coding questions (dry-run of your NQT code), Project explanation, DBMS (SQL joins, normalization), OOPS concepts, and basic DSA.
2. **Managerial Round (MR)**:
   - **Focus**: Situational questions, team management, project architecture, agile methodology, and puzzle solving.
3. **HR Round**:
   - **Focus**: Communication skills, relocation willingness, shift work flexibility, and verification of academic documents.
