# 📝 Reading Comprehension - Concept & Rules

> **Concept Definition**:
> Reading Comprehension evaluates a candidate's ability to read a passage, understand its core arguments, identify the author's tone, infer implicit details, and answer specific textual queries.

## 📊 Key Rules / Concept Table

| Grammatical Concept / Rule | Application / Example |
| :--- | :--- |
| Scanning Method | Read the questions first to locate key nouns/phrases in the passage quickly. |
| Skimming Method | Read the first and last paragraphs to grasp the primary thesis and tone of the passage. |
| Inference Rule | An inference must be directly supported by the text, not an extrapolation or generalization. |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Read the following passage:
'Technology has transformed the job market, making manual coding skills essential for many positions. While some argue that AI will make programmers obsolete, others believe it will serve as a tool that enhances developer productivity rather than replacing them.'
*Question*: What is the main theme of the passage?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Skim the passage. It highlights technology changes and the role of AI in coding.
Step 2: Compare views. It mentions two opposing views about AI and programming jobs.
Step 3: Analyze the options. The passage focuses on the debate surrounding AI's impact on developers.
Step 4: Answer: The impact of AI on programming jobs as a productivity tool.

### Example 2:
**Question**: From the same passage: What can be inferred about the future demand for coding skills?

**Solution**:
Step 1: Locate text about coding skills: 'manual coding skills essential for many positions'.
Step 2: AI is viewed as an enhancer: 'enhances developer productivity'.
Step 3: Deduce: Coding skills remain relevant, but the nature of the job will shift toward AI collaboration.

### Example 3:
**Question**: Identify the tone of the author in the passage.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Check for emotive or biased language. The passage presents both sides neutrally ('While some argue... others believe').
Step 2: Define tone: Objective, balanced, and informative.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Candidates often choose options based on their external general knowledge rather than the information provided strictly in the passage. If the text says the sky is green, then for the purpose of the exam, the sky is green.

## 💡 Clue / Shortcut Trick

🔥 **Shortcut**: Eliminate options that contain extreme words like 'always', 'never', 'only', 'all', or 'none'. Correct answers in RC are usually moderate and qualified.

## 🔗 Relation to Other Topics

💡 Tests general intelligence, analytical skill, and critical thinking required for comprehension and parsing complex program specifications.
