# 📝 Error Spotting - Concept & Rules

> **Concept Definition**:
> Error Spotting requires identifying grammatical mistakes in a sentence. It covers syntax errors, subject-verb agreement issues, incorrect tenses, modifier misplacement, and parallel structure errors.

## 📊 Key Rules / Concept Table

| Grammatical Concept / Rule | Application / Example |
| :--- | :--- |
| Subject-Verb Agreement | Singular subjects take singular verbs; plural subjects take plural verbs. (e.g. 'Each of the servers is (not are) running'). |
| Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement | Pronouns must match the noun they replace in number and gender. |
| Parallelism | Items in a list must have the same grammatical form (e.g. 'coding, testing, and deploying' not 'coding, testing, and deployment'). |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Identify the error: 'Neither the manager nor the developers was present at the meeting.'

**Solution**:
Step 1: Identify the subject: 'Neither... nor...'.
Step 2: Apply the proximity rule: when using neither/nor, the verb agrees with the closer subject.
Step 3: 'developers' is plural, so 'was' must be 'were'.
Step 4: Error is 'was'.

### Example 2:
**Question**: Identify the error: 'He has been working on this module since three hours.'

**Solution**:
Step 1: Identify time phrase: 'three hours' is a duration/period of time.
Step 2: Recall rules: 'since' is used for a point in time (e.g., 9 AM); 'for' is used for a duration.
Step 3: Change 'since' to 'for'.
Step 4: Error is 'since'.

### Example 3:
**Question**: Identify the error: 'The CPU performs calculations quick and efficiently.'

**Solution**:
Step 1: Analyze structure: 'performs calculations' is modified by 'quick' and 'efficiently'.
Step 2: 'quick' is an adjective, but we need an adverb to modify the verb 'performs'.
Step 3: Change 'quick' to 'quickly' to match the adverb 'efficiently' (parallelism).
Step 4: Error is 'quick'.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Overlooking the proximity rule for 'either/or' and 'neither/nor'. Candidates often assume the verb should agree with the first noun listed rather than the closer one.

## 💡 Clue / Shortcut Trick

🔥 **Shortcut**: First, find the subject and verb of the sentence. 80% of errors lie in the Subject-Verb Agreement or Verb Tense.
