# 📝 Error Spotting - Comprehensive Verbal Guide

> **Concept Definition**:
> Error Spotting tests core English grammar rules. TCS NQT questions present a sentence divided into parts or ask you to identify the grammatically incorrect sentence/segment from options.

## 📊 Grammar & Sentence Structural Rules

| Rule Index | Core Verbal Principle | Practical Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rule 1** | Subject-Verb Agreement: Collective nouns (team, jury) take singular verbs when acting as a unit, plural when acting individually. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 2** | Pronoun Antecedent: Indefinite pronouns like 'each', 'everyone', 'someone' take singular pronouns and verbs. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 3** | Dangling Modifier: Participial phrases must clearly modify the subject of the main clause. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 4** | Parallelism: Lists of items or verbs must share the same grammatical form (e.g. all gerunds or all infinitives). | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 5** | Tense Consistency: Avoid shifting from past to present tense mid-sentence without logical indicators. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 6** | Preposition Errors: Avoid using redundant prepositions (e.g., 'discuss about' or 'order for' are incorrect). | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 7** | Conjunctions: Use correct pairs like 'either...or', 'neither...nor', 'not only...but also'. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 8** | Adjectives vs Adverbs: Modify nouns with adjectives, and verbs/adjectives with adverbs. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 9** | Conditional Sentences: If + past perfect, would have + past participle (e.g. 'If I had studied, I would have passed'). | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 10** | Since vs For: 'Since' refers to a specific starting point in time; 'For' refers to a duration. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 11** | Scarcely/Hardly: Must be followed by 'when', not 'than' (e.g., 'Hardly had I arrived when it started raining'). | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 12** | No Sooner: Must be followed by 'than', not 'when' (e.g., 'No sooner did I leave than it poured'). | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 13** | Superior/Inferior/Prior: Must be followed by 'to', not 'than' (e.g., 'superior to'). | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 14** | One of the + Plural Noun + Singular Verb: e.g., 'One of the students is missing.' | Standard NQT verbal item |

## Previous Year Questions

### Q1. Identify the segment containing the grammatical error:  
*The team of engineers (A) / have successfully designed (B) / the prototype within the deadline (C) / No error (D).*  
**Answer**: `B`  
**Explanation**: The subject of the sentence is "The team" (singular), not "engineers". Therefore, the singular verb "has" must be used instead of the plural verb "have". The corrected sentence is: "The team of engineers has successfully designed..."

### Q2. Find the error:  
*If he would have worked harder (A) / he would have cleared (B) / the exam easily (C) / No error (D).*  
**Answer**: `A`  
**Explanation**: In third conditional sentences, the condition clause uses the past perfect tense ('had + verb'), not the conditional verb ('would have'). The correct phrasing is: "If he had worked harder, he would have cleared..."

### Q3. Find the error:  
*She is senior than (A) / all other colleagues (B) / in this department (C) / No error (D).*  
**Answer**: `A`  
**Explanation**: Comparative adjectives ending in '-ior' (senior, junior, superior, inferior) are followed by the preposition "to", not "than". Correct form: "She is senior to..."

### Q4. Find the error:  
*Not only did he lose (A) / his wallet, but (B) / also his important documents (C) / No error (D).*  
**Answer**: `B`  
**Explanation**: To maintain parallel structure, the parts of the correlation "not only... but also" must modify equivalent grammatical units. Correct form: "He lost not only his wallet but also his important documents."

### Q5. Find the error:  
*Each of the students (A) / were given a certificate (B) / by the chief guest (C) / No error (D).*  
**Answer**: `B`  
**Explanation**: "Each of" is followed by a plural noun but takes a singular verb because the subject is "Each". Therefore, "were" should be replaced with "was".
