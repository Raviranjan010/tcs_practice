# 📝 Synonyms & Antonyms - Concept & Rules

> **Concept Definition**:
> Synonyms are words with identical or very similar meanings. Antonyms are words with opposite meanings. These questions test your vocabulary size and contextual semantic understanding.

## 📊 Key Rules / Concept Table

| Grammatical Concept / Rule | Application / Example |
| :--- | :--- |
| Root Word Method | Break words down into prefixes (e.g., 'bene' = good, 'mal' = bad) to infer meanings. |
| Contextual Guessing | Substitute options in the sentence to see which one fits the overall tone and direction. |
| Tone Match Rule | A positive word will have a positive synonym and a negative antonym. |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Find the synonym of 'Meticulous'.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Define 'Meticulous': very careful, paying attention to tiny details.
Step 2: Match options like 'Scrupulous', 'Diligent', 'Precise'.
Step 3: Answer: 'Precise' or 'Scrupulous'.

### Example 2:
**Question**: Find the antonym of 'Benevolent'.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Break down root: 'bene' means good/kind.
Step 2: Opposing meaning must be bad/unkind/evil ('malevolent').
Step 3: Answer: 'Malevolent'.

### Example 3:
**Question**: Find the synonym of 'Ephemeral'.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Define 'Ephemeral': lasting for a very short time.
Step 2: Match words like 'transient', 'short-lived', 'fleeting'.
Step 3: Answer: 'Transient'.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Choosing the synonym when the question asks for the antonym (and vice versa). TCS NQT purposefully includes the synonym as an option in antonym questions to bait distracted candidates.

## 💡 Clue / Shortcut Trick

🔥 **Shortcut**: Check options polarity. If three options are negative words and one is positive, the odd one out is highly likely to be the correct answer (especially in antonym questions).
