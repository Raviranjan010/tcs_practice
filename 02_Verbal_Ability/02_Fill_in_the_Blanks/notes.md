# 📝 Fill in the Blanks - Concept & Rules

> **Concept Definition**:
> Fill in the Blanks tests vocabulary depth and grammatical precision. Sentences have one or more missing words that must be filled with the correct parts of speech (prepositions, conjunctions, verbs, adjectives).

## 📊 Key Rules / Concept Table

| Grammatical Concept / Rule | Application / Example |
| :--- | :--- |
| Preposition Rules | e.g. 'adhere to', 'comply with', 'different from', 'responsible for' |
| Tense Consistency | Ensure the blank matches the time frame (past/present/future) of the rest of the sentence. |
| Conjunction Clues | 'Although' / 'But' signal contrast; 'Therefore' / 'And' signal agreement. |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: The developer had to comply ________ the security policies of the bank.

**Solution**:
Step 1: Identify the verb: 'comply'.
Step 2: Recall the preposition pairing: 'comply' is always followed by 'with'.
Step 3: Answer: 'with'.

### Example 2:
**Question**: She was so ________ in her coding that she did not notice the fire alarm.

**Solution**:
Step 1: The sentence describes a high level of focus.
Step 2: Look for synonyms of absorbed or engrossed.
Step 3: Answer: 'engrossed' or 'absorbed'.

### Example 3:
**Question**: Although the software was buggy, the team ________ to launch it on time.

**Solution**:
Step 1: 'Although' signals contrast: buggy vs. launch success.
Step 2: Choose a verb showing successful effort despite hurdles.
Step 3: Answer: 'managed' or 'decided'.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Students often pick options that sound correct in their colloquial spoken English but violate formal grammar rules (e.g., using 'different than' instead of the grammatically correct 'different from').

## 💡 Clue / Shortcut Trick

🔥 **Shortcut**: Use the positive/negative tone method. If the sentence has a positive context, eliminate options with negative meanings.
