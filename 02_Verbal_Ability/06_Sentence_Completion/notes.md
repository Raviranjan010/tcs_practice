# 📝 Sentence Completion - Concept & Rules

> **Concept Definition**:
> Sentence Completion questions require filling in blanks to construct a grammatically correct and logically coherent sentence. It tests grammatical structure, verb-tense usage, and transition words.

## 📊 Key Rules / Concept Table

| Grammatical Concept / Rule | Application / Example |
| :--- | :--- |
| Clause Agreement | If the first clause is in the past perfect ('If he had studied'), the second clause must use 'would have' ('he would have passed'). |
| Logical Connectors | Match cause-and-effect relationships: 'Since', 'Because', 'So', 'As a result'. |
| Verb Pattern Rules | e.g. 'Avoid doing' (gerund), 'Decide to do' (infinitive). |

## ✍️ Worked Examples (Step-by-Step)

### Example 1:
**Question**: Complete the sentence: 'If the server had been backed up daily, we ________ our data during the crash.'

**Solution**:
Step 1: Identify conditional clause type: past perfect ('had been backed up').
Step 2: Apply conditional rule: past perfect requires 'would have + past participle'.
Step 3: Fill blank with 'would not have lost'.
Step 4: Answer: 'would not have lost'.

### Example 2:
**Question**: Complete the sentence: 'She decided to postpone the deployment ________ the critical bugs were resolved.'

**Solution**:
Step 1: Postponement is done for a purpose.
Step 2: Choose a conjunction showing time boundaries ('until').
Step 3: Answer: 'until'.

### Example 3:
**Question**: Complete the sentence: 'Not only did they finish the coding early, ________ they also resolved all lint errors.'

**Solution**:
Step 1: Spot correlative conjunction: 'Not only'.
Step 2: Pair it with its mandatory counterpart: 'but'.
Step 3: Answer: 'but'.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ **Pitfall**: Mixing up conditional forms, such as pairing past perfect with simple conditional ('If I had known, I would go' instead of 'I would have gone').

## 💡 Clue / Shortcut Trick

🔥 **Shortcut**: Scan for key pairs like 'Not only... but also', 'Hardly... when', 'Scarcely... when', 'Either... or', 'Neither... nor'. They resolve sentences instantly.
