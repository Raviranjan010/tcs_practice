# 📝 Sentence Completion - Comprehensive Verbal Guide

> **Concept Definition**:
> Sentence Completion evaluates structural flow and vocabulary. Sentences contain multiple subordinate clauses where the correct word must be chosen to complete the logic.

## 📊 Grammar & Sentence Structural Rules

| Rule Index | Core Verbal Principle | Practical Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Rule 1** | Look for contrast indicators: 'although', 'despite', 'yet' shift the logic direction. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 2** | Look for cause-and-effect indicators: 'because', 'therefore', 'consequently' maintain the same direction. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 3** | Verify tense agreements in active and passive clauses. | Standard NQT verbal item |
| **Rule 4** | Eliminate options that make the sentence grammatically incorrect (e.g. singular subject with plural verb). | Standard NQT verbal item |

## Previous Year Questions

### Q1. Despite the team's best efforts, the project failed because of _____ budget planning and _____ leadership.
A) meticulous/strong  
B) deficient/erratic  
C) robust/weak  
D) adequate/capable  
**Answer**: `B`  
**Explanation**: "Despite" and "failed because of" indicate that the reasons for failure must be negative. "Deficient" (lacking) and "erratic" (unreliable) are both negative qualifiers that explain the failure.

### Q2. The scientist argued that science is not a static body of knowledge, but a _____ process of discovery.
A) permanent  
B) dynamic  
C) fixed  
D) stagnant  
**Answer**: `B`  
**Explanation**: The contrast connector "but" signals that the word in the blank must be the opposite of "static". "Dynamic" (active, changing) is the direct antonym of static.
